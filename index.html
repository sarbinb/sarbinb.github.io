San Francisco

San Francisco likes to start its day late, so it's best to start your day like a local--with coffee--while the city wakes up. SF has a ton of small, boutique coffee shops throughout the city and our top picks are Philz and Blue Bottle. You can't really go wrong with diving into any coffee or bakery you see. Next up it's time to head over to Pier 39 and see the Sea Lions. Grab a sourdough bread-bowl soup at Boudin's, sample chocolate at Ghirardelli, and an Irish Coffee at the Buena Vista. Ride the train up to Lumbar St, then head over to Hayes Valley to do some shopping and see an upscale neighborhood. You can next head over to China Town, visit a coffee shop and see street art in the Mission district or see the Golden Gate Bridge. If you have time, head to Ocean Beach and if you walk far enough down, you can find some sand dollars. For dinner head over to San Tong's for Asian fried chicken and green beans (there's always a line).

Wine Country

Napa and Sonoma are beautiful places. Napa is a must because of the name and beauty, but Sonoma is a lot less commercial, intimate, and often cheaper tasting fees. Napa Valley has many small towns in the valley, and you can stop by any of the Chamber of Commerce's which gladly hand out buy-one-get-one samples. If you own a Visa Signature card, check out their list of wineries that waive the $15 tasting fee. Brian's favorites: St. Clements, Sterling, and Alpha & Omega. Also make sure to stop and enjoy the cute downtown of St. Helena. In Sonoma, Healdsburg and the Dry Creek Valley is very pretty and the wineries are super relaxed and you'll easily make a new friend. Grab a bite to eat at the Dry Creek General Store or bring some bread and cheese for a winery picnic (make sure the winery allows them)!

Pacific Coast Highway

Everyone always gets surprised when I tell them to spend their 3rd day driving. I can't say enough how beautiful this trip is and how much you'll enjoy the coastal views. Start your day headed down to Santa Cruz and see the boardwalk. Next, head down to Monterey and Carmel. If you have time the Monterey Bay Aquarium is amazing, and if not, walk around Cannery Row. Next up, head down towards Big Sur and get ready to pull over for breathtaking ocean views driving along the cliffs. Drive through the redwoods in Big Sur and head to Julia Pfeiffer Falls for a waterfall cascading onto the beach. Next, head to Nepenthe for a sunset on the cliffs.

Be a San Jose Local

San Jose and the South Bay isn't the first place you think to explore, but there are a lot of really cute cities and downtowns that are great to explore. Some interesting places to visit: Palo Alto's University Street (visit C.R.E.A.M.!), Stanford, Campbell downtown, Saratoga downtown, Los Altos downtown, or Los Gatos downtown. If you'd like to visit Esther and my church, they have service on Saturday nights and Sundays at Menlo Park. Of course, you can always check out the Google campus to visit the Android statues and grab a bite to eat at Shoreline Lake Marina. If you still want some wine or pretty views of the South Bay, head up to Mountain Winery and Ridge Winery. You can also stop by downtown San Jose at J. Lohr Winery. If you like micro-brews, check out Original Gravity. For meals check our our food list or grab some street food from San Padro Square.

Breathtaking Nature

If you have the time, we would highly recommend you take the long drive to Yosemite or Tahoe. These places are some of the biggest, cleanest, best nature. You go to these places and are transported into America 300 years ago. Both Yosemite and Lake Tahoe are 4 hour drives. Yosemite takes a little more planning, and if you're thinking of going there expect either a really long day or booking a place now. If you can't find a place in Yosemite, you can try to stay in one of the cities along the way, such as Buck Meadows or Tracy. Lake Tahoe has easier accommodations to book, and you can go visit the casinos on the Nevada side of the lake. Make sure to stop by Emerald Bay and drive around the lake.
